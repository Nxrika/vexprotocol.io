Vex Protocol Litepaper

1. Introduction: The Volatility Gap in DeFi

Digital asset markets have matured at an incredible pace, but there's still a critical piece missing: a reliable, transparent source for implied volatility data. Traditional finance has long depended on volatility indices like the VIX to price derivatives, assess market stress, and create hedging strategies. DeFi operates without this essential benchmark.

While existing price oracles show where the market stands today, they don't capture forward-looking volatility expectations. This leaves builders without the foundational metric needed for mature derivatives markets. Vex solves this by delivering a complete on-chain volatility standard purpose-built for decentralized finance.

2. The Vex Protocol: System Architecture

Vex introduces a decentralized volatility oracle that calculates and publishes the CVX Index directly on-chain. By aggregating data from major liquidity sources across options and perpetual futures markets, Vex constructs a market-driven measure of implied volatility that reflects true market expectations.

The protocol delivers a robust, manipulation-resistant standard that developers can trust when building advanced derivative products, hedging strategies, structured yield systems, or risk management tools. Vex serves as essential infrastructure for the next generation of decentralized derivatives.

3. CVX Methodology: How It Works

The CVX Index adapts Model-Free Implied Volatility (MFIV) principles to the unique crypto-derivatives landscape. Unlike historical variance models or proprietary calculations, CVX captures market expectations by integrating observed option prices across a continuous strike range.

This approach extracts implied variance from a complete set of out-of-the-money options, creating an index that reflects true implied volatility as determined by the market itself‚Äînot by any single protocol or pricing assumption.

4. MFIV Calculation Process

CVX computation follows a systematic, transparent process:

  1. Collect live quotes from major option markets
  2. Filter and cleanse data to remove stale or illiquid values
  3. Construct interpolated volatility surfaces for BTC and ETH
  4. Integrate implied variance across strikes using modified MFIV
  5. Adjust results using perpetual futures basis and funding rates
  6. Aggregate, validate, and publish the index on-chain

These steps ensure CVX remains accurate, resilient, and resistant to manipulation attempts.

5. Data Sources & Market Inputs

CVX reliability depends on high-quality, liquid market data from carefully selected sources:

  • Deribit BTC & ETH Options
  • Lyra On-Chain Options
  • Perpetual Futures Funding Rates from major venues
  • Perpetual Futures Basis data for BTC and ETH

These sources were chosen for consistency, liquidity, and long-term market relevance. Additional venues will be integrated as decentralized options markets mature.

6. Update Frequency & Data Delivery

The CVX Index updates every 60 seconds, balancing real-time responsiveness with gas efficiency. Off-chain data services continuously process incoming quotes and produce signed variance calculations.

Vex validators verify payloads and push updated CVX readings on-chain. This architecture ensures DeFi protocols can rely on CVX as a stable, highly available volatility reference feed suitable for pricing, hedging, and risk modeling.

7. On-Chain Security & Verification

To maintain data integrity, Vex implements multiple security layers:

  • Validator quorum submitting signed CVX values
  • Median aggregation to prevent outlier manipulation
  • Timestamp and signature verification in smart contracts
  • Immutable storage of all historical index readings

This system ensures transparency and prevents any single actor from meaningfully influencing the index.

8. VEX Token Utility

The VEX token (not yet deployed) will secure and govern the protocol with several key functions:

  • Staking: Validators must stake VEX to participate, creating economic guarantees for honest behavior
  • Governance: Token holders vote on model parameters, validator onboarding, and system upgrades
  • Fee Distribution: Protocols using CVX pay fees distributed to stakers
  • Ecosystem Incentives: Early validators, builders, and integrators receive token rewards
  • Treasury Development: Portion of supply dedicated to maintenance, audits, and grants

Final supply structure and distribution will be published before token deployment.
